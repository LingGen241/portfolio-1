About Me

I'm Huy, a 19-year-old Informatics student at the University of Washington with a passion for turning complexity into practical solutions. I thrive at the intersection of technology and collaboration, always eager to learn new tools and frameworks. My goal is to build systems that empower others and make information accessible.

What I Do

I guide projects from concept to completion, aligning stakeholders and leading teams with a clear vision. By breaking work into manageable sprints, I keep progress transparent and adaptable. Along the way, I champion continuous learning and shared ownership to drive meaningful results.

My Methodology

I live by an Agile mindset. I iterate quickly, gather feedback, and refine relentlessly. Empathy and open communication guide everything I do at work and in life. I don’t believe in "work-life balance"—to me, work is life, and life is work. If I ever feel the two are separate, it means I’m doing something wrong. Instead, I weave my passion for problem-solving, collaboration, and human-centered design into every moment, ensuring each solution is secure, user-friendly, and built to last.

  • I was born and raised in Saigon’s vibrant south, but childhood visits to my dad’s family in Taiwan immersed me in traditional Chinese values and shaped my maturity.
  • In my free time, you’ll find me weight-training at the gym. It keeps me in peak shape and fuels the stamina I need to move fast in life.
  • I’m a perpetual learner: whether I’m studying Chinese, diving into self-development books, or watching growth-mindset videos, I’m always leveling up both as a PM and in life.
